2D Design and Cutting

Part 1: 3D Modeling an Object from Real Life

For this assignment, we were tasked with creating a couple of CAD model objects, with a joint of some sorts. The first object I created was a water bottle.

Picture of the water bottle, along with the initial sketches.

To create this model, I divided the water bottle into 3 parts: the bottle, the lid, and the handle. Below are sketches of the components:

From there, I extruded the features and used a combination of chamfer and fillet tools to modify the bodies.

Raw bodies of the bottle, followed by the refined and more accurately modeled curved model.

Finally, I added my joint to attach the handle to the lid, and to attach the lid to the bottle. To do this, I created projections of the circles on the end of the handle onto the cap to give a point of reference for the joint.

I also added a couple extra details, such as the engravings on the bottom of the bottle and the bottle being functional (theoretically I could pour CAD water into the hollow inside of the bottle :P).
